# Gap Map — Vision

> **Gap Map is where curiosity becomes understanding** — a single place where the
> messy signals of *what people struggle with*, *what they wish existed*, and
> *what research already knows* come together so you can learn from them and act
> on them.

## Why this exists

I built Gap Map for myself, for two reasons that turned out to be the same thing:

1. **To truly understand users and where the real market gaps are** — not by
   guessing, but grounded in what people actually say across the web.
2. **To finally have one proper home for research learning** — reading papers is
   a hobby of mine, and I was tired of losing what I learned across scattered
   tabs, bookmarks, and half-read PDFs.

Along the way the most valuable part wasn't any single source — it was
*connecting* them. Seeing a painpoint people complain about line up with a
feature they wish for, and a paper that quietly already points to the answer.

I learned an enormous amount doing this. The vision is to turn that into
something that helps anyone trying to understand a space deeply — and to share
what we learn so others don't have to start from zero.

## What I believe

- **Evidence over opinion** — every insight traces back to a real source you can open and read.
- **Connection over collection** — the value is in the relationships between signals, not the size of the pile.
- **Learning is the product** — the goal isn't just answers; it's understanding that compounds over time.
- **Built to be shared** — knowledge is more useful when it's organized, reusable, and passed on.

## What it helps you do

- **See the real gaps** — painpoints people have that nothing solves well.
- **Hear the voice of the user** — across communities, app reviews, and forums, in their own words.
- **Keep a living research library** — papers, claims, and learnings, organized and searchable, not forgotten.
- **Ask and learn** — cited, source-aware answers ("what do the papers say vs. what users say"), with a thread you can follow into the knowledge graph.
- **Share what you find** — maps, briefs, and learnings others can build on.

## Who it's for

Anyone who wants to understand a space deeply:
- **Founders & builders** chasing the right gap to solve.
- **Product people** who want evidence behind decisions.
- **Researchers & lifelong learners** building a durable knowledge base.
- **The curious** — people who learn by connecting dots.

## Where it's going

From a **personal research companion** → to a **shared knowledge space** where
understanding is collected once, kept fresh as the world changes, and passed on —
so learning about people, products, and research becomes something we do
together, not alone.
